"Goonj" Mural

The full mural painted for “Goonj! Being Brown in Chinatown” at Centre A Gallery in Vancouver for Indian Summer Festival.

The mural depicts the story of the Komagata Maru, a ship carrying 376 passengers from Punjab to BC in 1914 but was not allowed to dock on Vancouver shores due to racist exclusion laws that were designed to keep out Asian Immigrants. The scene depicts the passengers throwing bricks at the police after being told to sail back to India.

The life-sized cutouts are of 2 passengers aboard this ship along with Baba Gurdit Singh who’s quote is above: “…The time for prayer & puja is past… This is the time to pick up the sword….”

The UC Berkeley Campanile is on the far right, which signifies the militant legacy of resistance against British colonizers lead by Kartar Singh Sarabha in Berkeley pictured above with pen & rifle. These two elements represent armed revolution & mobilizing the people through poetry & the Ghadar Newspaper.
